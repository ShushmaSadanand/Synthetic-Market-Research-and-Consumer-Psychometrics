## Project: Synthetic Dataset Modeling & Spatial Wayfinding Analysis (McDonald's Minimalist "M")
**File:** `Synthethic Datasets - MI.pdf` (Quantitative Behavioral Research Deck)

A state-of-the-art quantitative marketing study utilizing a generated synthetic sample of $N = 500$ participants (perfectly split 50/50 between localized urban residents and incoming tourists). The project models behavioral visit likelihood variations when exposed to a high-clutter traditional food advertisement versus a radical, minimalist spatial "M" directional billboard.

### Core Data Science & Psychometric Insights
* **The Minimalist Design Premium:** Statistically validated that reducing visual clutter directly enhances cognitive processing speeds. Exposure to the Minimalist Billboard (Ad A) significantly outperformed the Traditional Food Advertisement (Ad B), boosting overall consumer visit intent by **+12.8%** ($M_{Minimalist} = 5.38$ vs. $M_{Traditional} = 4.77$ on a 1–7 Likert Scale).
* **Dual-Segment Behavioral Flywheels:** Dissected the synthetic cohort to uncover two distinct, uncorrelated psychometric drivers explaining *why* the minimalist billboard acts as a behavioral conversion catalyst:
  * **The Tourist Cohort $\rightarrow$ Risk Mitigation Engine:** Marked by high Dining Risk Aversion ($M = 5.66$). Discovered a powerful positive correlation ($r = 0.29$) between unfamiliar city anxiety and brand familiarity. For tourists, the clean "M" serves as an immediate **Beacon of Safety** against local dining uncertainty.
  * **The Local Cohort $\rightarrow$ Efficiency Optimization Engine:** Marked by intense Convenience Sensitivity ($M = 5.12$). Discovered a commanding positive correlation ($r = 0.39$) between speed requirements and directional signals. For residents, the simple geometry operates as functional **Urban Infrastructure** for rapid, low-cognitive-load confirmation.
* **Methodology & Variable Matrix:** The simulated experimental architecture tracked:
  $$\text{Independent Variable (Ad Layout Variant [A vs. B])} \times \text{Psychometric Covariates (Risk Aversion, Convenience Sensitivity)} \longrightarrow \text{Dependent Variable (Visit Likelihood [1-7 Likert Scale])}$$
